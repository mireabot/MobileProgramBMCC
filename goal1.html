Build a Comprehensive and Experiential Secure Mobile Programming Concentration at BMCC

BMCC will build on existing degree programs and courses to establish a robust curricular track for mobile app development with emphasis on cybersecurity, secure coding, and experiential learning linked to real-world opportunities with NYC-based companies. BMCC students on this track will be prepared with core technical skills and competencies necessary for ongoing study at a four-year college and/or employment in the NYC mobile programming industry.

  1. Create 3 new courses and modify 3 existing courses to be offered by the BMCC CIS department. Receive approval from the BMCC Curriculum Committees by 5/30/2023
  2. Create a fully outfitted mobile programming lab and associated curricula, to be established by Fall 2023
  3. Design Fall and Spring semester programming workshops on secure mobile programming to be offered to any enrolled CIS students. Launch workshop by Spring 2023
  4. Annually expose at least 25% of enrolled BMCC CIS students to secure mobile programming curricula or experiential learning opportunities by the 2023 academic year
  5. Build a cohort of at least 10 total students who are concentrating in secure mobile programming by the 2023 academic year
  6. Encourage all students concentrating in secure mobile programming to obtain industry accepted certifications by offering a certification readiness workshop during Fall and Spring semesters. Workshop to be established by January 2023
  7. Offer internship opportunities to at least half of the program participants during summer break. Target at least 10 students each year
  8. Organize industry expert presentations and fire-chat meetings. Target 2 per academic year starting in the Spring 2023 semester
  9. Offer stipends of $500 to each student, with total at least 5 students each school year to attend one professional conference related to mobile programming, secure coding, or the NSF ATE conference

Revised and New Courses

BMCC will develop three new courses and revise three existing courses aimed at Computer Information Systems (CIS) majors. These courses will introduce students to background skills and knowledge necessary for pursuing further training in developing secure mobile applications. These courses will emphasize engaging students in solving real-world problems and making connections between foundational concepts and industry opportunities. Project faculty will work closely with both the BMCC CIS department and the Advisory Board to refine the scope of these courses

  • iOS Mobile Programming
  • Mobile Cybersecurity Design Concepts
  • Capstone Mobile App Development

Creation of Mobile Programming Lab

BMCC will outfit one of our existing computer labs with tablets that will be used as a test bed for mobile programs that are developed on PCs in a simulation environment. These tablets will remain permanently located in the lab in special charge docks and will be available to students who need to test their mobile applications with a physical platform

Workshops and Mentoring to Support Mobile Programming Certification for Students

Students who complete mobile programming and/or iOS mobile programming courses will be encouraged and supported in obtaining industry mobile programming certification. Various certifications are available, such as Google Developers Certification and App Development with Swift Certification. The project team will provide test preparation workshops for these certifications. In these workshops, instructors will orient students to the tests and sample questions as well as share with students their working experiences in mobile development

Secure Mobile Programming Club Activities

A new faculty-mentored club will be created for BMCC CIS students, with weekly meetings hosted in the Mobile Programming Lab. Students will be engaged in dialogue about mobile applications, challenges, and career opportunities. The faculty mentor will invite alumni working in mobile programing and related fields to visit and present about their career trajectories and technical projects. Faculty will arrange a visit by the club to a local tech company and the students have a chance to meet with a complete mobile development team and understand their working environment, job responsibilities, work schedule, company culture and the future employers expectations. The visit will allow students to connect the classroom knowledge to real world problems and it will motivate students learning. Under the supervision of the faculty mentor, students will work on a small mobile development project that will be showcased by the CIS department

Industry Internships

BMCC is located at the heart of the Manhattan financial business district with many top 100 firms having offices in the area. BMCC and CIS department faculty have longstanding relationships with many of the education officers in these companies and there is a strong track record of partnering around student internships through the BMCC Office of Internships and Experiential Learning (OIEL). BMCC understands that professional internships and work experiences are at the heart of workforce training, and the staff of OIEL has agreed to support placement of mobile programming students in high quality industry internships either with Cognizant, the industry partner on this proposal, or with comparable NYC-based employers such as local non-profit organizations (HealthFirst, NYC Geospatial Information System and Mapping Org, etc.), tech and financial companies (Bank of New York, IBM and Bloomberg), or local IT companies (LarrosaTek). Given the number of collaborations between BMCC CIS faculty and the NYC tech sector, we do not foresee barriers to meeting our target metric for internship placement (at least 5 students each year from 2023-2025)

Industry Expert Presentations and Fireside Chats

BMCC will organize at least one visit each semester by industry experts to offer presentations and/or fireside chat panels. These events will be open to high school students and teachers from the partner schools, enrolled BMCC students, faculty, and alumni. This exposure to the perspectives of industry experts will allow for valuable networking opportunities as well as student insight into challenges and career opportunities in the field

Participation in BMCC Career Day

Alumni and industry partners will be invited (in-person or virtually) to participate in annual Career Day at BMCC. Both enrolled BMCC students and high school students engaged in pipeline activities will be encouraged to attend and, where applicable, to showcase their work in a poster exhibit. This important professional networking event is already part of the BMCC calendar, and we will leverage it to ensure that students are exposed to professional networks and opportunities in secure mobile programming sector in NYC

Attendance of Industry Conferences by Students

To broaden their exposure to mobile development, students who work with faculty in the Mobile Programming Club and/or have been placed in industry internships will be encouraged to submit a project abstract to at least one professional conference. If accepted, students will receive stipends to attend. Students who are not presenting but are keenly interested in attending will also be eligible for a stipend. BMCC has set the target of supporting at least 4 students each academic year in attending and/or presenting at a conference, where they will have opportunities to meet with industry professionals and researchers and to gain deeper understanding of mobile programming and real-world problems/solutions

Academic Advisement Workshops

Leveraging resources for the BMCC Academic Advisement Center, CIS faculty will conduct one workshop each semester to assist students on the secure mobile programming track in developing meaningful educational plans that will further their academic, career, and personal goals. All students will also be offered 1:1 faculty mentoring and career counseling support
