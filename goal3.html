Develop Faculty Skills to Sustain ATE Investments

BMCC will create opportunities for department faculty to improve their skills in mobile programming and secure coding, as well as in teaching in these areas, in order to achieve strong buy-in and preparedness to undertake the project and sustain it into the future.

  1. Train at least 4 faculty members in mobile programming during the first and second years of the project
  2. Continue to provide training annually to any new faculty as well as additional skill building with existing faculty in the program, with a commitment to ensuring at least 4 faculty are prepared each semester to steward and deliver secure mobile programming curricula and to advise students
  3. Provide financial support for 4 program faculty to attend an ATE conference once per year
  4. Organize yearly Stakeholder Advisory Board meetings to discuss topics of interest in secure coding and mobile programming, and to review BMCC curricula in light of emerging trends

Training of Faculty in new Mobile Programming Applications

Faculty training will be available through BMCCs long-time training partners including Learning Tree International. This highly reputable training resource offers both in person and online courses in mobile programming and secure coding as part of a wide spectrum of Information Technology topics. Participating faculty will be offered free vouchers to use for trainings of their choice at a time that is most suitable to their schedule

Conference Attendance by Faculty

The project team and other interested faculty will attend and present curricula and lessons learned from this project at various secure coding and mobile development conferences aimed at both higher education and industry audiences. Conference participation will allow team members to learn the latest mobile development technologies and real-world applications as well as pedagogical innovations and tools. These will also prove to be invaluable networking events as BMCC faculty continually pursue expanded internship opportunities for BMCC students. At least 4 faculty will present to share their research findings in this project at conferences each year, with financial support provided

Faculty and Industry Roundtables

The project team will organize yearly roundtable meetings to convene CIS faculty, interested educators from the partner high schools, and industry professionals to discuss topics of interest in cybersecurity and mobile programming

  1. Year 1 (2022-2023)
    • Choose 4 faculty members to steward the mobile programming courses
    • Offer training opportunities to interested faculty members
    • Organize yearly meetings
  2. Year 2 (2023-2024)
    • Choose 4 faculty members to steward the mobile programming courses
    • Offer training opportunities to interested faculty members
  3. Year 3 (2024-2025)
    • Choose 4 faculty members to steward the mobile programming courses
    • Offer training opportunities to interested faculty members
