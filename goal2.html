Extend the Experiential Learning Pipeline to Manhattan High School Students

Partnering with a local high school, BMCC will extend its college-to-career experiential learning pipeline to high school students by: (1) exposing them to applications and opportunities in the exciting field of secure mobile programming through outreach; (2) inviting them to build foundational skills and gain confidence through participation in summer STEM programming at BMCC; and (3) ensuring students are aware of opportunities to further technical training on the pipeline through enrolling in BMCC.

  1. Organize an event with high schools to be held at the beginning of each school year aimed at exposing teachers and students to BMCC STEM program offerings for high school students and opportunities to enroll after graduation and pursue training in secure mobile programming. First event will be held in September 2023
  2. Increase the number of high school students taking programming courses and earning college credits through the BMCC College Now program, which gives high school students a head-start with pursuing college courses. Additionally, coordinate with the BMCC College Now office to create a course for college credit that is dedicated to mobile programming. College Now (CN) is a collaborative program of the City University of New York (CUNY) and the New York City Department of Education (NYCDOE), serving over 700 students annually. This existing mechanism will facilitate transfer into the BMCC CIS department after graduation.   3. Design and launch a Summer Secure Mobile Programming Workshop for high school students with priority given to students from the partner schools. The workshop will span 2 weeks with activities on 4 days of each week (4 hours per day). Target launch in Summer 2023 with the goal of enrolling at least 5 students in the first year and increasing to 15 students in year 3
  4. Participate in existing BMCC annual Career Day to promote the Mobile Programming concentration starting from the Spring Semester of 2023

High-School Presentations and Meetings

BMCC will visit the partner high schools campus once per semester (with virtual visits as needed due to COVID-19) in order to build awareness among high school students of career options in Computer Science, in general, and in secure mobile application development, specifically. Students will have opportunities to ask questions and engage in dialogue with BMCC faculty

High-School Programming Workshops

A BMCC faculty-led workshop will be organized once per semester to provide a hands-on learning experience for interested high school students to work on real programming applications and gain understanding of foundational concepts and skills in the process. We will seek to include at least one industry professional in each workshop as a strategy for illustrating the real-world relevance of the project, and to reinforce information about career paths. Students from e high school students. An important outreach strategy, these workshops will be offered both in person and/or virtually to allow more enrollment. Interested high school students will receive follow-up information about additional opportunities to learn at BMCC through the Summer Programming Camp and through enrollment in College Now courses and matriculation after graduation

Summer Programming Camp

The project team will work with industry partners to offer a Summer Programming Camp for high school students starting from 2023. The camp will run for 2 weeks each summer (4-days per week and 4 hours each day). The camp will be facilitated by a faculty mentor paired with an industry mentor, who will guide students through the experiential project of developing a simple mobile game. Students will complete the camp with a basic understanding of the software development life cycle, quality assurance, and secure coding. With an emphasis on engagement and fun, the Summer Programming Camp will also serve as a gateway into enrollment in BMCC courses and degree programs
